# Manuscript Outline

## Working title

Rapid Public-Health Risk Stratification for WASH Recovery in Gaza Using Open Humanitarian Data

## Abstract

Draft after data extraction.

## Introduction

Frame the study as public-health systems research focused on WASH access, environmental health, population vulnerability, health-service access, and recovery prioritization.

## Methods

### Design

Secondary-data public-health systems analysis.

### Setting

Gaza Strip.

### Data sources

Public humanitarian, WASH, health-system, and geospatial sources.

### Measures

- WASH service stress
- environmental-health hazard burden
- health-system access constraint
- population vulnerability
- operational access constraint

### Index construction

Use transparent domain scoring and classify areas into priority groups.

### Sensitivity analysis

Compare equal-weight, WASH-weighted, and health-access-weighted specifications.

## Results

Planned outputs:

- source inventory
- indicator availability table
- domain score table
- total priority score table
- sensitivity comparison
- priority map

## Discussion

Interpret findings as decision support for public-health planning and equity-oriented recovery sequencing.

## Limitations

Address secondary data limits, incomplete geography, changing dashboards, restricted data exclusion, missing individual-level outcomes, and noncausal design.

## Conclusion

Summarize how open data can support rapid public-health prioritization where WASH and health-system stress converge.
