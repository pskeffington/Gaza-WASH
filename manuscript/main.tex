\begin{abstract}
Water, sanitation, and hygiene disruption in conflict settings requires careful separation between verified assessment evidence, localized operational observations, infrastructure-recovery context, and analytic interpretation. This manuscript develops a source-gated Gaza WASH assessment framework that links household WASH evidence, local enumeration-area observations, time-stamped monitoring reports, infrastructure damage and recovery assessments, and public-health mechanism literature through an explicit claim-control matrix. The draft is structured so that every finding must trace to a controlled source identifier, use limitation, and manuscript-allowance status before it carries evidentiary weight. This version is a manuscript scaffold pending exact page-level source binding and final citation metadata.
\end{abstract}

\noindent\textbf{Keywords:} Gaza; WASH; conflict; public health; water infrastructure; sanitation; humanitarian assessment; source audit
