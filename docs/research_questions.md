# Research Questions

## Working title

**Rapid Public-Health Risk Stratification for WASH Recovery in Gaza Using Open Humanitarian Data**

## Primary research question

Can readily accessible humanitarian, WASH, health-facility, and spatial data identify high-priority areas in Gaza where WASH disruption and health-service constraints converge?

## Secondary research questions

### RQ1: WASH-system variation

How does WASH-system disruption vary across Gaza by administrative area, displacement site, or service catchment?

### RQ2: Environmental-health convergence

Where do WASH disruption, solid-waste accumulation, sewage exposure, stagnant water, pests, crowding, and displacement vulnerability converge?

### RQ3: Health-system access

Which areas combine high WASH or environmental-health burden with constrained access to functional health facilities, mobile clinics, or emergency medical services?

### RQ4: Priority-index robustness

Do area priority rankings remain stable when the index is scored with alternative weighting assumptions?

## Hypothesis frame

This project should avoid causal language in the first manuscript. Use the following language:

> Areas with greater WASH disruption and environmental-health hazards are expected to show higher public-health priority scores, particularly when health-service access is constrained.

## Non-goals for first manuscript

- Estimating individual disease risk.
- Attributing mortality or morbidity to specific infrastructure failures.
- Evaluating intervention effectiveness.
- Using restricted partner-only data.
- Making claims from unverified dashboard values.
